# Model Comparison Seminar Paragraph

We compared five supervised classification algorithms to evaluate whether kelp canopy decline was better captured by linear additive effects, nonlinear decision boundaries, or tree-based feature interactions. Logistic Regression was used as a transparent linear baseline, SVM as a nonlinear margin-based classifier, and Random Forest, XGBoost, and LightGBM as tree-based ensemble models capable of capturing nonlinear relationships among canopy state, SST stress, and upwelling-related proxies. Models were evaluated under a temporal train-validation-test split to reduce look-ahead bias and better approximate forward prediction. Because the task is framed as early-warning screening, performance was assessed using PR-AUC, recall, F1, and false negatives rather than accuracy alone. In addition to comparing algorithms, we compared feature sets within each model class: canopy-only, OISST-only, and canopy+NOAA. This within-model comparison distinguishes the predictive contribution of direct biological state variables from NOAA environmental exposure context without claiming that NOAA variables caused kelp decline or replaced direct canopy monitoring.
